At the conclusion of the war, the United States rose from 16th most powerful miitary power to 1st, was the leading superpower and sole posessor of nuclear arms, and would grow to be the most powerful nation in human history.

Akune Brothers

The Akune family had 4 brothers who fought in World War II. Though they had all fought in the same war, they had not fought on the same side. Two had fought for the U.S. and two for Japan. Harry and Ken found the country in which they were born and to which they returned was becoming increasingly hostile to citizens of Japanese heritage. The two borthers were sent to an internment camp in colorado, where they were recruited by the US army. Ichiro, their father, quickly brokered a peace among his sons, and all four of the veterans returned to California and later fought on the same side (for the U.S.) in the Korean War.

SS v. wehrmacht

Only days before the wars end, The Battle for Castle Itter in the Austrian North Tyrol village of Itter was fought on May 5, 1945, in the last days of the European Theater of World War II. Troops of the 23rd Tank Battalion of the 12th Armored Division led by Captain John C. Lee, Jr., a number of Wehrmacht soldiers led by Major Josef Gangl, a Waffen-SS officer who defected, and French prisoners freed of war defended Castle Itter against an attacking force of a Waffen SS Division until relief from an American Infantry Regiment arrived. The French prisoners included former prime ministers, generals and a tennis star. It may have been the only battle in the war in which Americans and Germans fought side-by-side. Popular accounts of the battle have called it the strangest battle of World War II.

Tuskegee Airmen

The Tuskegee Airmen was a group of Air Force units composed primarily of African American pilots. The group compiled an impressive record, primarily in the Mediterranean Theater of Operations, despite facing frequent resistance to their presence in the formerly all-white Army Air Corps. Although the best-known Tuskegee Airmen were the fighter pilots of the 332nd Pursuit Group ,the 477th Bombard Group (the first black bomber group) was also part of the Tuskegee Airmen. Th airmen were known for impressive skill, such as the fact that in over 200 total missions, they had never once lost an escorted bomber to the enemy, being a first in World War II.
